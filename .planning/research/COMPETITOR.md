## Process Technology

**IFP Process:** Storage Tank → Settling Tank → Centrifuge → Vacuum Distillation → Adsorption → Filter → Storage

- **Key distinction**: Uses **adsorption** (acid clay / activated earth) — NOT hydrotreating
- Adsorption = Group I output (sulfur 0.2–1.2%, VI typically 80–95)
- Cannot produce Group II+ without capital-intensive hydrotreater addition
- BIS IS 18722 committee membership is for Group I RRBO standards only

## Santosh Competitive Advantages vs IFP

| Dimension | IFP | Santosh |
|---|---|---|
| **Product grade** | Group I RRBO (adsorption) | Group II+ RRBO (hydrotreating) — SUPERIOR |
| **Sulfur content** | 0.2–1.2% (Group I) | <300ppm target (Group II+) |
| **Technology** | Established but dated adsorption | REVA (Pune) with European hydrotreating |
| **BS-VI compliance** | Group I insufficient for BS-VI OEM specs | Group II+ meets BS-VI automotive standards |
| **EPR mandate fit** | Can supply EPR quota but at lower quality | Premium position for mandated 25%+ RRBO |
| **IOCL MOU (March 2026)** | Not specifically named | Validates exactly Santosh's Group II+ model |
| **Brand** | Established, cartoon mascot, broad | Clean slate — can build technical premium brand |

## Website Design Observations (ifp-petro.com)

- Heavy use of cartoon mascot (oil drop character "Go Green") — playful, not technical
- Broad service message, not product-specific
- No technical spec comparison vs virgin base oil
- Focuses on legacy/heritage positioning ("Since 1977", "Pioneer")
- **Gap for Santosh**: A technically precise, data-forward Group II+ positioning will differentiate strongly against IFP's broad heritage claims

## Strategic Implication for Santosh Website

**Do not compete on legacy — compete on technology and grade.**

IFP owns "first mover" and "most established." Santosh must own "technically superior" and "Group II+ specialists."

Key messages:
1. "Group I was sufficient for BS-IV. BS-VI needs Group II+." → IFP can't make this claim
2. "Hydrotreating removes what adsorption cannot." → Direct process superiority
3. "IOCL's own March 2026 MOU validates the Group II+ circular economy." → Policy tailwind
4. "Not just recycled — re-refined to Group II+ specification." → Quality positioning
