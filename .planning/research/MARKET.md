## Regulatory Framework

### EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility)
- **Regulation**: Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules 2016, amended 2023 — used oil classified as hazardous waste
- **EPR effective**: April 1, 2024
- **Targets**: 5% RRBO in lubricant manufacturing (FY2025) → scaling 5% annually → 50% by FY2031
- **Mechanism**: CPCB digital portal — lubricant producers/importers must buy EPR certificates from registered recyclers
- **Impact**: Forces organized collection and boosts formal re-refining volumes dramatically

### BIS Standards
- **IS 18722 (Part 1)**: India's first standard specifically for RRBO — stricter than Group I virgin specs in some parameters
- India is the **first country in the world** to have separate RRBO standards
- IFP is on the BIS PCD 25 committee formulating these standards (Group I focus)

### Key Recent Event
- **IOCL + Re Sustainability MOU (March 2026)**: Building India's first national Group II+ RRBO circular economy — directly validates Santosh's entire business model and Group II+ positioning

## Technology Landscape

### Key Re-Refining Technologies

| Technology | Process | Output Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vacuum Distillation + Hydrotreating** | REVA (Santosh) | **Group II+** | Best quality, highest capex |
| Vacuum Distillation + Adsorption | IFP | Group I | Common in India, lower quality |
| Thin-Film/Short Path Distillation | — | Group II/III | Efficient, minimizes degradation |
| Solvent Extraction + Acid Clay | — | Group I | Small-scale, older technology |

### Santosh Process (REVA Technology)
1. **Dehydration**: 120°C, removes water/glycol/light ends; OWS system for wastewater
2. **Vacuum Distillation** (Wiped Film Evaporator — WFE): Separates base fractions at 280–310°C; asphalt residue recovered as saleable byproduct
3. **Hydrotreating**: H₂-rich gas + metallic catalysts → removes S/N/halogens, saturates olefins and aromatics → Group II+ polishing
4. **Flash drum + stripping**: H₂ recovery, vapor purge to thermal oxidizer
5. **QC**: VI, sulfur, flash point, pour point verification
6. **Dispatch**: Bulk tanker / 210L drum / IBC

Base oil yield: 70–75% from feed

### Why Hydrotreating = Group II+
- Adsorption (IFP): Removes color and odor but CANNOT desulfurize or saturate aromatic structures → stays Group I (sulfur 0.2–1.2%, VI typically 80–95, poor oxidation stability)
- Hydrotreating (Santosh): Reacts sulfur → H₂S (removed), saturates aromatics → paraffins, achieves VI >95 and sulfur <300ppm → genuine Group II+

## BS-VI Automotive Standards & OEM Requirements

- India adopted BS-VI (Euro VI equivalent) from April 2020
- BS-VI lubricant formulations require Group II or Group II+ base oils — Group I base stocks are insufficient for many BS-VI engine oil grades (especially PCMO and HDO)
- Major OEMs (Maruti, Hyundai, Tata, Mahindra) increasingly specify Group II+ for factory fill
- This creates structural demand pull that Group I re-refiners like IFP CANNOT serve
