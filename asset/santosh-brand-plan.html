04 · Identity
Voice & Tone

Santosh speaks like a confident, forward-thinking sustainability report — not a brochure, not a spec sheet. Sentences are short and declarative. Data is cited. The future is assumed, not hoped for.

✓ Write Like This
"India produces 1.3 million tonnes of used lubricating oil every year. Only 15% is formally recycled. We are changing that."
"Our Group II+ RRBO meets virgin base oil specifications — verified by third-party lab testing."
"The circular economy for lubricants is no longer a future concept. At Santosh, it is our daily operation."

Short declarative sentences. Concrete numbers. Present tense. Confident without being boastful.

✗ Avoid This
"We are a leading company providing world-class solutions for all your petroleum recycling needs!"
"Our state-of-the-art facility uses cutting-edge technology to deliver superior quality products."
"We are committed to excellence and strive to provide the best service possible to our valued customers."

Vague superlatives. Filler adjectives. Passive constructions. Exclamation marks. These erode trust.

Confident

State facts. Don't hedge unnecessarily. "We produce" not "We aim to produce."

Forward-looking

Reference regulations, trends, and India's circular economy targets as context for your work.

Technical-honest

Buyers are engineers. Use Group II+, viscosity index, BIS specs — don't dumb it down.

Human

Workshop owners are also an audience. Keep the collection/supplier pages warm and accessible.
